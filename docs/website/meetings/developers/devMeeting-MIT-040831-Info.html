Date and Time

August 30 and 31 (Monday and Tuesday), 2004
9 AM to 4 PM each day

* Developers are encouraged to stay for the remainder of the week (September 1-3) for a coding session. A wireless internet connection will be available, but developers will need to bring their own laptop.  The purpose of this coding session is to give developers a chance to work together in person mostly on the coding of uPortal 3. What we do depends on how far along uPortal 3 code is at the time, any decisions made at the developers meeting, and the interests of those participating in the coding session.

Host and Location

Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT)
Cambridge, MA, USA
Building W92
Back Bay Room
(Room is reserved 8 AM to 6 PM each day)

Agenda

Discussion of uPortal 2.4 and uPortal 3:
Domain objects, Registries, Pluto implementation, WSRP4J, Groups and Permissions, Exception handling, Logging, Documentation, etc.
Detailed Agenda

Preparation

It is recommended that you review the following before coming to the uPortal developers meeting:
  • The overview document that outlines the major goals of uPortal 3.
  • uPortal 3 code that has been developed so far. You can find it in the CVS up3 sandbox module.
  • The Pluto portlet container
  • The WSRP4J framework
  • The JSR 168 Portlet Specification
  • The WSRP Specification
  • The Spring Framework, especially the concept of Inversion Of Control (IoC) and the Spring JDBC abstraction framework
  • The Sakai Project
  • Chuck Severance's presentation on the current proposed integration between Sakai and uPortal
  • A summary of the discussion at the last JA-SIG conference regarding the uPortal community process
